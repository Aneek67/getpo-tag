# Design System Strategy: The Digital Gallery

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
The Creative North Star for this design system is **"The Digital Gallery."** 

Unlike a typical e-commerce marketplace that feels cluttered and transactional, this system treats every comic book issue as a piece of fine art. We are moving away from the "grid of boxes" to an editorial experience that prioritizes negative space, cinematic scale, and a sophisticated silence. 

To break the "template" look, we utilize **Intentional Asymmetry**. Large-scale `display-lg` typography will often overlap high-fidelity character art, and product listings will use varying card heights to create a rhythmic, masonry-style flow. The goal is a layout that feels curated by a human eye, not generated by a database.

---

## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy
The palette is intentionally restrained to a monochromatic base, allowing the vibrant, CMYK-heavy colors of comic art to provide the only necessary pigment.

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Explicit Instruction:** Do not use 1px solid borders to define sections or containers. The "box" is dead. Boundaries must be defined exclusively through background color shifts. For example:
- A featured comic detail section (`surface-container-low`) sitting atop the main page (`surface`).
- Navigation bars defined by `surface-container-highest` with a backdrop blur rather than a bottom-stroke.

### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a physical stack of premium cardstock.
*   **Base Layer:** `surface` (#f9f9fb)
*   **Structural Sections:** `surface-container-low` (#f3f3f5)
*   **Interactive Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) to provide a "pop" of clean white against the off-white background.
*   **Elevated Overlays:** `surface-container-high` (#e8e8ea) for menus and modals.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To inject "soul" into the minimalism, use **Glassmorphism**. Floating navigation and quick-buy bars must use semi-transparent surface colors with a `backdrop-filter: blur(20px)`. 
*   **Signature Polish:** Use a subtle linear gradient for Primary CTAs, transitioning from `primary` (#0059b5) to `primary_container` (#0071e3). This prevents the "flat-button" look and mimics the premium depth of a physical high-end tech device.

---

## 3. Typography
We utilize a ratio-driven typographic scale to establish an editorial hierarchy.

*   **Display (SF Pro Display):** Use `display-lg` (3.5rem) for hero titles. Tighten the letter-spacing (-0.02em) to create an authoritative, "Apple-esque" headline feel.
*   **Headlines & Titles:** These are the "Gallery Labels." Use `headline-md` for issue titles. The contrast between a massive `display` title and a restrained `title-sm` metadata label creates a sophisticated tension.
*   **Body (SF Pro Text):** All functional text uses `body-md`. It is the workhorse of the system, optimized for legibility in product descriptions.
*   **Labels:** Use `label-sm` in all-caps with increased letter-spacing (+0.05em) for category tags (e.g., "MINT CONDITION," "SILVER AGE") to denote premium grading.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering**, not structural scaffolding.

*   **The Layering Principle:** To create a sense of importance, stack `surface-container-lowest` on top of `surface-container-low`. This creates a soft, natural "lift" that guides the eye without visual noise.
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If an element must float (like a "Buy Now" FAB), use an extra-diffused shadow: `box-shadow: 0 20px 40px rgba(26, 28, 29, 0.06)`. The shadow must be almost imperceptible, mimicking soft studio lighting.
*   **The "Ghost Border" Fallback:** In rare cases where a border is required for accessibility, use the `outline_variant` (#c1c6d6) at **15% opacity**. It should be a suggestion of a line, not a hard boundary.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** High-gloss. Gradient from `primary` to `primary_container`. `xl` roundedness (1.5rem). No border.
*   **Secondary:** Glass-style. `surface_container_highest` with 40% opacity and backdrop blur.
*   **Tertiary:** Text-only using `on_surface_variant` with a chevron icon for "Learn More" style interactions.

### Cards (The "Issue" Card)
*   **Constraint:** Forbid divider lines within cards.
*   **Style:** Use `surface-container-lowest` with `lg` (1rem) corner radius. Separate the comic cover art from the title using a `spacing-4` (1.4rem) vertical gap. The art should have a very subtle `0.5rem` radius to match the card, creating a "nested" harmony.

### Inputs & Search
*   **Search Bar:** Instead of a white box with a gray border, use a pill-shaped (`full` roundedness) `surface-container-high` background. Text should be `body-md`. 
*   **Focus State:** Shift background to `surface-container-highest` and add a 2px `primary` glow with 20% opacity.

### Navigation (The Curator Bar)
*   A top-centered or bottom-anchored floating bar. Use a heavily blurred glass effect. No icons should have labels; use high-quality, thin-stroke iconography that speaks for itself.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
*   **Do** use `spacing-20` (7rem) or `spacing-24` (8.5rem) for vertical section padding. Let the layout breathe.
*   **Do** let comic art bleed off the edge of the screen in hero sections to imply a larger world.
*   **Do** use "Tonal Hover" states: when hovering over a card, change the background from `surface-container-lowest` to `surface-bright`.

### Don't
*   **Don't** use pure black (#000000) for text. Always use `on_surface` (#1a1c1d) to maintain a premium, ink-on-paper feel.
*   **Don't** use 1px dividers to separate list items. Use `spacing-3` (1rem) of white space or a subtle background tint change.
*   **Don't** use standard "drop shadows." If it doesn't look like ambient gallery lighting, it doesn't belong.
